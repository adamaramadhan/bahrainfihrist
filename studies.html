Studies

This section contains bibliographic information about secondary sources and scholarly studies related to Bahraini history. Click on each category to explore the sources.

  • Crawford, Harriet (1998). Dilmun and its Gulf Neighbours. Cambridge University Press.

    Comprehensive study of the ancient Dilmun civilization in Bahrain and its connections to Mesopotamia and the Indus Valley.

  • Lombard, Pierre (1999). Bahrain: The Civilization of the Two Seas. Immel Publishing.

    Overview of archaeological discoveries in Bahrain highlighting its role as a maritime crossroads.

  • Potts, Daniel T. (2009). The Archaeology and Early History of the Persian Gulf. Oxford University Press.

    Archaeological analysis of the Persian Gulf region with significant sections on ancient Bahrain.

  • Khuri, Fuad I. (1980). Tribe and State in Bahrain. University of Chicago Press.

    Analysis of the transformation of tribal structures and the development of the modern state in Bahrain.

  • Lawson, Fred H. (1989). Bahrain: The Modernization of Autocracy. Westview Press.

    Study of political development in Bahrain from the late 19th century to the 1980s.

  • Zahlan, Rosemarie Said (1998). The Making of the Modern Gulf States. Ithaca Press.

    Comparative study including Bahrain's political evolution in the context of other Gulf states.

  • Al-Rumaihi, Muhammad Ghanim (1976). Bahrain: Social and Political Changes since the First World War. University of Durham.

    Analysis of Bahrain's economic transformation from pearl fishing to oil production.

  • Fuccaro, Nelida (2009). Histories of City and State in the Persian Gulf: Manama since 1800. Cambridge University Press.

    Urban history focusing on Bahrain's capital city and its commercial development.

  • Lawson, Fred H. (2022). The Economy of Bahrain. Routledge.

    Comprehensive analysis of Bahrain's economic development from traditional industries to modern finance.

  • Holes, Clive (2001). Dialect, Culture, and Society in Eastern Arabia. Brill.

    Sociolinguistic study of Bahraini Arabic dialects and their cultural context.

  • Al-Tajir, Mahdi Abdalla (1987). Language and Linguistic Origins in Bahrain. Kegan Paul International.

    Study of Bahraini linguistic heritage and its historical development.

  • Al-Naqeeb, Khaldoun Hasan (1990). Society and State in the Gulf and Arab Peninsula. Routledge.

    Sociological analysis of traditional and modern social structures in Bahrain and the wider Gulf region.

  • Al-Khalifa, May (1999). Charles Belgrave: Biography and Diaries. Bahrain Historical & Archaeological Society.

    Biographical study of the British advisor to the rulers of Bahrain from 1926 to 1957.

  • Zahlan, Rosemarie Said (2002). The Al Khalifa Family and the Modern History of Bahrain. Ithaca Press.

    Historical analysis of Bahrain's ruling family and their role in the country's development.

  • Joyce, Miriam (2012). Bahrain from the Twentieth Century to the Arab Spring. Palgrave Macmillan.

    Includes biographical studies of key political and cultural figures in modern Bahraini history.
