Studies

This section contains scholarly works, articles, books, and research papers that analyze and interpret Bahrain's history. Click on each category to explore the studies within.

  • Al-Nabi, Mohammad Noor. The Historical Geography of Bahrain. Manama: Historical Documents Centre, 2012.

    Analyzes geographical factors in Bahrain's historical development.

  • Vine, Peter. Pearls in Arabian Waters: The Heritage of Bahrain. London: Immel Publishing, 1986.

    Examines Bahrain's maritime geography and its influence on pearl diving industry.

  • Al-Tajir, Mahdi Abdalla. Bahrain 1920-1945: Britain, the Shaikh and the Administration. London: Croom Helm, 1987.

    Includes biographical information on key political figures in Bahrain.

  • Belgrave, Charles. Personal Column. London: Hutchinson, 1960.

    Memoir of the British advisor to the rulers of Bahrain from 1926 to 1957.

  • Khuri, Fuad I. Tribe and State in Bahrain. Chicago: University of Chicago Press, 1980.

    Analyzes the relationship between tribal structures and state formation in Bahrain.

  • Lawson, Fred H. Bahrain: The Modernization of Autocracy. Boulder: Westview Press, 1989.

    Examines political development in Bahrain during the late 20th century.

  • Al-Khalifa, Abdullah bin Khalid and Michael Rice. Bahrain through the Ages: The History. London: Kegan Paul International, 1993.

    Contains chapters on economic developments in Bahrain's history.

  • Rumaihi, Muhammad Ghanim. Bahrain: Social and Political Change since the First World War. London: Bowker, 1976.

    Examines economic transformations in modern Bahrain.

  • Holes, Clive. Dialect, Culture, and Society in Eastern Arabia. Leiden: Brill, 2001.

    Examines linguistic and cultural aspects of Bahraini society.

  • Al-Mubarak, Khalid. Cultural Traditions in Bahrain. Manama: Ministry of Information, 2005.

    Overview of Bahraini cultural practices and their historical origins.
