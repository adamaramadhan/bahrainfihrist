Primary Sources

This section contains bibliographic information about primary sources related to Bahraini history. Click on each category to explore the sources.

  • Al-Ahsa'i, Ahmad ibn Zayn al-Din (1815). Tarikh al-Bahrayn wa-al-Ahsa.

    Manuscript preserved in the Bahrain National Museum. One of the earliest comprehensive historical accounts of Bahrain and the Al-Ahsa region.

  • Al-Nabhani, Muhammad ibn Khalifah (1892). Al-Tuhfah al-Nabhaniyah fi Tarikh al-Jazirah al-Arabiyah.

    Historical chronicle focusing on the Arabian Peninsula with substantial sections on Bahrain.

  • Ibn Battuta (14th century). Rihlat Ibn Battuta.

    The famous traveler's account includes descriptions of Bahrain during his journey through the region.

  • East India Company (1763-1850). East India Company Records on Bahrain.

    British Library, London. Collection of correspondence, reports, and agreements pertaining to British interests in Bahrain.

  • Lorimer, J.G. (1908-1915). Gazetteer of the Persian Gulf, Oman and Central Arabia.

    Comprehensive British intelligence report with detailed information on Bahrain's geography, history, and tribal politics.

  • India Office Records (1820-1947). Political and Secret Department Files on Bahrain.

    British Library, London. Collection including political reports, administrative correspondence, and treaties related to Bahrain.

  • Belgrave, Charles (1960). Personal Column.

    Memoir of the British advisor to the rulers of Bahrain from 1926 to 1957.

  • Bent, Theodore and Mabel (1890). Southern Arabia, Bahrain, and Ancient Settlements in the Persian Gulf.

    Archaeological and ethnographic observations from the Bents' travels in the region.

  • Palgrave, William Gifford (1865). Narrative of a Year's Journey Through Central and Eastern Arabia.

    First-hand account of Bahrain and its pearl industry in the mid-19th century.

  • Glob, P.V. (1954-1970). Danish Archaeological Expeditions to Bahrain.

    Field reports and findings from the pioneering Danish archaeological missions to Bahrain.

  • Rice, Michael (1985). Dilmun Discovered: The Early Years of Archaeology in Bahrain.

    Documentation of early archaeological work in Bahrain with primary source material.

  • Bibby, Geoffrey (1969). Looking for Dilmun.

    Personal account of archaeological discoveries in Bahrain by a member of the Danish expedition.

  • Al-Tajir Family Archives (18th-20th centuries). Merchant Records and Correspondence.

    Private collection. Business records, letters, and agreements of a prominent Bahraini trading family.

  • Bahrain Court Records (1920-1960). Legal Documents and Case Files.

    Bahrain National Archives. Collection of legal proceedings showing social and economic aspects of daily life.

  • Pearl Diving Contracts (1850-1930). Collection of Commercial Agreements.

    Bahrain National Museum. Documents detailing the organization and financing of pearl diving expeditions.
