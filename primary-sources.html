Primary Sources

This section contains original historical documents, manuscripts, and firsthand accounts related to Bahrain's history. Click on each category to explore the sources within.

  • Al-Bahrani, Yusuf. Lu'lu'at al-Bahrayn. Ed. Muhammad Sadiq Bahr al-'Ulum. Najaf: Matba'at al-Nu'man, 1966.

    A seminal biographical dictionary of Bahraini scholars from the 13th century CE.

  • Ibn Battuta. Rihlat Ibn Battuta. Beirut: Dar Sadir, 1964.

    Contains descriptions of Bahrain during the author's travels in the 14th century.

  • Lorimer, J.G. Gazetteer of the Persian Gulf, Oman and Central Arabia. Calcutta: Superintendent Government Printing, 1908-1915.

    Contains detailed information about Bahrain in the early 20th century.

  • Palgrave, William Gifford. Narrative of a Year's Journey Through Central and Eastern Arabia (1862-63). London: Macmillan and Co., 1865.

    Includes observations on Bahrain during the author's travels.

  • British Political Agency Records, Bahrain. India Office Records, British Library.

    Archive containing correspondence and reports relating to Bahrain, 1900-1947.

  • Annual Reports on the Persian Gulf. Foreign Office Records, The National Archives, UK.

    Contains yearly assessments of political and economic developments in Bahrain.

  • Glob, P.V. Danish Archaeological Investigations in Bahrain. Copenhagen: Gyldendal, 1957.

    Documentation of early archaeological excavations in Bahrain.

  • Rice, Michael. The Archaeology of the Arabian Gulf. London: Routledge, 1994.

    Contains chapters on archaeological findings in Bahrain.
